Spider-Man

Spider-Man, a beloved superhero, is the alias of Peter Parker, a teenage superhero from New York City. After being bitten by a radioactive spider, Peter gains extraordinary powers including wall-crawling, superhuman strength, and spider-sense. With his motto "With great power comes great responsibility," Spider-Man is known for his agility, intellect, and dedication to protecting his city.

Real Name: Peter Parker

First Appearance: Amazing Fantasy #15 (1962)

Origin

Peter Parker was a high school student who gained superpowers after being bitten by a radioactive spider. Initially using his powers for personal gain, Peter's life changed when his uncle Ben was killed by a criminal he had earlier allowed to escape. This tragedy inspired Peter to use his powers for good, adopting the superhero identity of Spider-Man.

Abilities and Gadgets

  • Spider-Sense: An extrasensory ability that alerts Peter to danger.
  • Wall-Crawling: Ability to cling to and crawl on walls and ceilings.
  • Superhuman Strength: Enhanced strength that allows him to lift heavy objects and overpower foes.
  • Web-Shooting: Using web-shooters, Peter can shoot webs to swing between buildings and trap enemies.

Key Storylines

  • Spider-Verse: A crossover event featuring multiple versions of Spider-Man from different dimensions.
  • Death of Jean DeWolff: Peter grapples with his role as Spider-Man while investigating the murder of his friend, Jean DeWolff.
  • Infinity War/Endgame: Spider-Man joins the Avengers in the battle against Thanos, showcasing his bravery and growth as a hero.

Personal Life

  • Family: Raised by his Aunt May and Uncle Ben Parker. His biological parents were Richard and Mary Parker.
  • Love Interest: Mary Jane Watson, a key figure in Peter's personal and superhero life.
  • Allies: Frequently teams up with other heroes like Iron Man, the Avengers, and the Fantastic Four.

Fun Facts

  • First Appearance: Amazing Fantasy #15 (1962), created by Stan Lee and Steve Ditko.
  • Web-Shooters: Peter Parker invented his own web-shooting devices, unlike other heroes with natural web abilities.
  • MCU Impact: Portrayed by Tom Holland, Spider-Man is a central figure in the Marvel Cinematic Universe.
